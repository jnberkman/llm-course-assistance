What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
TAKE. IT. Literally amazing, and I love love lovveeeeedddd my teacher!!!
I’d really recommend this class to students who want to improve their french overall, but particularly their speaking and oral skills, as it’s highly discussion–based. It’s not too difficult, and I found it pretty easy to get consistently high grades, just by paying attention in class and completing all of my homework on time. The best part about the class is that there is no final exam, which really takes the pressure off during finals season.
If you like learning languages this class will be perfect for you!
It's a great class, with interesting material about media and current events. The workload is not high, and you get to have plenty of fun discussions about the francophone world. Overall, I loved my experience in French 30.
If you've taken French 20, go ahead and take French 30. It's a great next step, that will concretize the key grammar points and leave you more confident in your speaking and writing abilities.
Although it is taught in French, this class is a lot less scary than it seems. There are plenty of chances to ask questions and participate in group discussions. Comprehension of other people speaking comes quickly as there are a lot of opportunities to listen.
I would tell future students that the classroom discussions are what you make of them. Make sure to practice speaking as much as you can in class! Don't be afraid of making mistakes. The assignments don't take long (podcasts and essays), but the deadlines can creep up on you so make sure to write them all out in advance.
Take it! Even if you feel like your French isn't sufficient, try it out! You may surprise yourself.
