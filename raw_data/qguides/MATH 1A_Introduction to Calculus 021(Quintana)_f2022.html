What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Although the course seemed very easy for most people, it was because most people had taken some type of Calculus class before. For people who did not have any Calculus experience, it seemed very challenging for them as the class style was "learning by doing" so it seemed to hone problem solving skills. However, due to the minimal resources and lack of formal traditional teaching, it did not seem to be an adequate true introduction to Calculus for people who had never learned it before. Despite that, the assessments are very fair as they mimc the practice assessments and psets pretty well and are a pretty good representation of your proficiency. There are also reassessments for every learning objective, so you essentially have the opportunity to reassess every question, which is a pretty nice because it gives you a chance to learn from your mistakes.
This course is more challenging than you'd expect of an intro calc class. I took AP calc AB (and did well) and still found this course very challenging. My peers who didn't have prior experience with calc did not find their experience with this course to be very manageable given the amount of assumed knowledge. I'd say that this course is a bigger time suck than expected as well, given that it has 3 Psets a week. Your experience in this course is completely dependent on your TF assignment. I will say this: if your TF is bad switch right away (and some of the ones teaching this course are indeed bad)!
This course is difficult! If you were placed into a high math, I suggest just taking that one because this class overcomplicates basic calculus. Essentially what was covered in AP Calc from high school was covered in this class in the weirdest possible order. The assessments are kind of difficult, but they are doable if you study everything they give you as a resource. Use the videos on the Canvas and go to MQC. MQC is especially helpful for completing PSETS. I feel like MQC is the only way you can really complete the PSETs and get good scores on them. Also, beware that the quality of section leaders varies tremendously: if you want to collaborate with your friends/classmates, it will be pretty difficult because you'll soon realize that you all learn the same thing in different way or that you didn't even learn what they learned in class. The perk of the class are the amount of resources for their students, but the execution of teachings is not that good.
There is a lot of time spent working on problems set and preparing for tests during the week. Also, we have to do math projects, which I found Unhelpful and inapplicable to the concepts we were learning. it just felt like extra and unnecessary work.
You need some level of calculus knowledge before this course. Fast passed but students who haven't taken calculus in awhile (ie. veterans) should not be discouraged if once strong in math.
I was very excited to study a wide array of subjects and topics at Harvard, but after this class, I don't think I will take a math course here again.
If you take AB Calc, this class is a breeze and doesn't really require any brain power
