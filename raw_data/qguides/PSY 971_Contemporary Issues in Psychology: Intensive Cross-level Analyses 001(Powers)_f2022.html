The course overall is a relatively dull concentration requirement that I don't feel I got much out of. However, my TF Ellen was fantastic –– she made every class extremely engaging and helped our 8–person section become comfortable and close. She also did a great job helping me narrow down my topic for a final paper into something that I truly enjoyed researching and learning about.
This class is a really great way to practice reading and writing psychology (almost like a concentration specific Expos). That being said, if you want to get something out of the course you should do all of the readings and take time early in the semester to be intentional with reading your sources for your final paper because you will need them for every assignment. If you are a Psych concentrator, I know you don't really have a choice whether to take this class or not, but you shouldn't be worried because it is totally doable!
If you have to take this course, I definitely recommend Annie! She’s amazing! She gives excellent feedback on everything and she also is understanding on deadlines and classes. This course is very writing heavy, so be prepared to write an assignment almost every week.
Wonderful introduction to psychological research! Really enjoyed getting the chance to think more closely about psychological writing and the final essay writing process includes extensive instructor support.
This is a required course for a psychology concentration so there really is no way to get out of it. The class itself is not bad but it *really* depends on who you get as your TF. My TF was luckily on the nicer side and she also provided a lot of useful feedback about our assignments. I know some other people in other sections that had very different experiences. Basically, your goal is to learn to write in a way that your TF will like and the best way to do that is to get to know your TF and their preferences. Some of the assignments take longer than expected so keep that in mind –– the good thing is that they offered 4 free extensions so those came in really handy.
The more you put in the more you get out
Have fun with coming up with your mock experiment for the final paper :)
Be open, be vulnerable, and develop a relationship with your TF...this will help in the long run.
Don't let the rubric or "Sophomore Tutorial" label scare you away from this course—while it can be tedious, PSY971 is what you make of it. The course assignments, readings, and overall material can feel like busy work and was mainly the reason why I labeled this class Difficult; it was less so concerning the difficulty of material as much as it was about the amount of hours I had to put into doing well. That being said, the course timeline and purpose can definitely improve your writing if you put in effort—coming out of PSY971, I feel much more confident in my ability to analyze an article in less than 5 minutes (understanding, summarizing, listing pros/cons/limitations). More importantly, I've come out of this course a far better writer than coming in.

If you have the opportunity, I cannot stress this enough—PLEASE CHOOSE ANNIE–LORI JOSEPH AS YOUR TF!!!!! That woman is iconic. She is the reason for my progress and I can attribute my success in this course and in psychology writing to her. The way the course is set up really helps you near the end of the semester, because the mini–assignments are essentially the final paper dissected into smaller organs. I saved hours on the final paper just from slapping on things I found or wrote on my Annotated Bibliography. More importantly, Annie was amazing at providing constructive criticism, and always pushed me to think more critically about my analysis for an article or my work for the paper. She asked a lot of prodding, open–ended questions that I believe to have partially contributed to the quality of my paper. I chose to pursue a longitudinal study, which I believe none of my classmates were doing, and she gave me support throughout the entire process because it wasn't common and also quite difficult in hindsight. She also facilitated discussions in section, which was really helpful. Overall, she is one of the best TFs I have had hands down and I am grateful to have learned from her!
It's a requirement, so you likely have to take it. That said, it was a great introduction into the psychology concentration. You get to know the students and instructor really well and you'll come on a lot in the way you read and write in the discipline.
I definitely recommend taking this class after Psy 1 and at least one foundational course you are interested in if you are sure that you want to concentrate in psychology because it definitely allows you explore any topic in psychology that you are passionate about with weekly readings, discussion, and writing assignments like the sophomore essay. The 15 page sophomore essay sounds daunting at first, but the instructors do an excellent job of guiding you throughout the process with assignments like the annotated bibliography and outline that prepare you for this final essay. The only suggestion I have to improve this class is to change the days that writing assignments are due from Sundays to Mondays because it is not considerate to assign essays on the weekend. Other than that, my tutorial leader was always willing meet during the week and provided thorough feedback on each assignment.

This class has a lot of deadlines and small assignments to complete. Make sure to stay organized and plan out your work time as well as when you'll use the extensions allotted to you. Also, there is a decent amount of reading for each class so make sure to carve time out for that.
Honestly, I was pretty skeptical about this course at first because I generally haven't heard the best things about sophomore tutorials. But I was so presently surprised by this course! It was definitely one of my top courses of the semester, not the least because of Dr. Levari's teaching. Her enthusiasm for the subject is infectious, and she has the perfect balance of being kind and approachable while also being organized and knowing her stuff really well. I was pretty intimidated at first because I haven't really had formal research experience, but this course made it so digestible I didn't even notice how much I was learning and growing each week. For me, this tutorial really helped me confirm that psych is the right path, so if you're even remotely thinking about declaring, I would definitely take this course. (Even if you don't declare, you'll still learn so so much in terms of how to design an effective study, how to read and extract information from research papers, and also a bunch of random cool psych stuff.)
You will spend an absurd amount of time writing, editing, and working on assignments in this course. For me, I spent more time on this course weekly than any other class at Harvard just because of the sheer amount of assignments that are seemingly due every week.
I loved this class!! I learned so much every week. Our class had a great community and interesting discussions every week. The class builds up to a capstone paper at the end, which feels very fulfilling to write.
