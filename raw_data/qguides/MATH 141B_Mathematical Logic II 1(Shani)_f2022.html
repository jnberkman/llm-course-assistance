What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is great!! I really think taking Mathematical Logical will give you a great, new perspective on Math that you cannot get from other courses, I highly recommend!
The material is quite different from the first semester of Mathematical Logic (141A), but equally as fun and engaging. A lot of what you will learn in the first half of the course has important consequences for the theoretical foundations of computer science (e.g. Turing's incompleteness theorems), and the second half delves into some of more of the recent discoveries in Ramsey Theory (e.g. Paris–Harrington principle), which currently has lots of active research going on.

Get ready to be comfortable with Peano arithmetic – the kinds of structures that it allows, and the kinds of statements that can (or can't) be proven from its axioms. And fear not, for your old friend, infinite finitely–branching trees, will be back in action (this time, you'll learn how to code them)!
This is a light class, ideal for those who are curious about the subject. It starts off slow, but the results are worth it.
If you are mathematically curious in any way, you MUST take this course. It is hands down the most interesting class I have taken at Harvard and I am honestly devastated to have to move on from my two semesters taking logic because it was so fun. There isn't much prior knowledge you need coming into the class –– just eagerness to work and a bit of experience writing proofs. Combinatorics, set theory, and number theory obviously would be helpful but are not necessary. The class is challenging but in a way that is motivating and engaging. The difficulty does not lie in the content itself, but in expressing your ideas in the formal language and communicating exactly what you want to say in this weird language of logic and math symbols. I cannot stress enough how great this class was –– if I could take it over again I would.
