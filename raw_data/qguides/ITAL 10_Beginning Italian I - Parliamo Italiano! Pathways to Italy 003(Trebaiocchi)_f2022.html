Take this class! I came into Italian 10 with no previous Italian experience and I left feeling comfortable speaking with my classmates and professors in Italian in class. Going into the class, it can be jarring that it's taught largely in Italian, but I believe this really helped me to learn the language at a faster pace than I have with previous languages. This class is amazing, highly recommend.
It's SCARY starting out, but this class is for real awesome. Highly highly highly recommended.
This is definitely an intro language class so it meets often (4 times a week) but it was really fun and we actually learned a lot of italian, and the small classes are fun.
If you're even somewhat considering taking Italian (and have little to no background in the language) TAKE THIS CLASS!!! The workload is very manageableâ€”you should expect about 1 hour of homework each night, but the homework is usually online exercises from the textbook which makes it really fun. to complete, so it really isn't something to be concerned about. You'll have an exam every other week or so, and these exams are relatively low stakes and the grading is very reasonable, so long as you demonstrate growth and knowledge in the concepts that were being covered. You'll also have a take–home assignment every other week, and these assignments can be a bit time consuming but they really help you get better at writing in Italian. There's also no final exam for the class (which is SO nice during finals season) and instead you have to create a final presentation on any topic of your choice relating to Italian culture, which is super fun and an overall great way to end the course. The teaching team does a great job at ensuring all assignments are both engaging and enriching, and there wasn't a single assignment in this class that I didn't enjoy completing. There is also so much support from the teaching team for any students that are struggling, and there are numerous opportunities for extra credit throughout the semester. Most extra credit opportunities involved your attendance at an Italian social gathering or cooking night, which is clearly a win–win. I also really want to pub my section leader, Giulia Sbaffi, who was the absolute BEST throughout the entire semester. Not only is she great at teaching italian in an engaging way through her awesome Canva slideshows, but she became a mentor and a friend to all of the students which made a fun and supportive learning environment. I entered this course knowing little Italian, and I left having a solid level of fluency where I feel like I could navigate most situations during a study abroad in Italy. Take this class!!!
