Listen, this class is HARD. I only took it because of major requirements and unless you're a math person, don't put yourself through this unless you have to. Let me tell you some information to know before taking this class:
1) There's no lecture, only sections. You'll only learn in small groups. This could be a pro or con depending on how you look at it. I personally wish there was a lecture so we could have a unified baseline understanding of the topics, but the sections are good because you get to ask questions and get to know your TF/CAs much better.
2) There are 3 PSETs a week (due Monday, Wednesday, and Friday) with QRD assignments spread throughout the semester (due on Fridays). This is NOT to be underestimated. It's a lot of work (especially the first couple of PSETs, those were rough).
3) GO. TO. THE. MQC!!!!!!! This is literally the only way I've been able to do well in this course. The CAs are gods amongst men and genuinely the only reason I understand anything that's going on in class. Plus, it's the best way to get good grades on your PSETs.
4) Form a PSET group ASAP. It makes your life so much more bearable when doing these homework assignments.
5) AP Calc BC did not prepare me well for this class and I know many of my fellow (public school) peers feel the same way. Be prepared to relearn math in a way that you've never learned math before.
Before taking this class, make sure that you have the fundamentals of calculus learned thoroughly. The course moves along very quickly so it's important that you don't fall behind. Office hours and the MQC are great tools to use for this course, it's very helpful to do PSets alongside other students.
Math 1B is a challenging class, but it does a great job preparing you for higher–level math in college. It teaches you to think critically, applying math concepts to solve problems in ways you have never thought about before.

GO TO MQCs!!!!! But try solving the PSETs on your own first and then going to MQCs for help.
GO TO MQC. IT SAVES YOU SO MUCH TIME TO DO PSETS THERE. AND MAKE SURE YOU GET THERE EARLY BEFORE IT'S PACKED. It starts at 7:30pm, but get there early, around 7pm is best.
