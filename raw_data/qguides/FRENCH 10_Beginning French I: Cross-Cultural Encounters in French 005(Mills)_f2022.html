This class is designed to help you become more proficient in real–world conversational French. If this is a goal you have, I would recommend taking this course. By the end, I could speak most of what was on my mind in basic French.

However, the class is not an "Easy A." You will not be able to blend in with the wallpaper and walk away with an A. If you want that grade, it is challenging at times. The main concern people have is just how friendly this course is to beginners. As with all beginner language courses here at Harvard, this course can be rough in the beginning if you have no experience with the language. I would say to review some basic vocabulary before starting the class; this will help you a lot.

To get a good grade in the class, there are a few things you need to know.

First, quizzes. Study for the quizzes, and study for them in a particular way. Instead of just looking at the review guide before the quiz, be sure to practice by writing out what is on it. This is crucial, because on the quizzes, they will mark you down if you write an accent mark going the wrong direction. Mind every detail, because the small mistakes will. stack up and worsen your grade.

Next, compositions. These are 250–word writings you have to do. These are a big part of your grade too. Start them early because 250 words in a foreign language can be a lot. And keep in mind––these essays are pretty harshly graded. Even down to the accent marks! Don't be discouraged; it just takes time to review your own work.

Lastly, oral examinations (graded interviews pretty much). These are also a sizable chunk of your grade. My advice is to practice speaking as much as possible and be familiar with the vocabulary that will be tested. Having a partner to practice with helps, but don't feel like you must have a partner to practice. Just rehearse with yourself and you'll do just as well.

Overall, this class can take up some time if you are striving for an A. But even that process is not too bad if you stay on top of things. Take this class if you want to buckle down and improve your French.
