Take this class if you have to but be ready mentally to deal with a lot of Psets, QRDs, weekly problems and 2 midterms. Start your Psets early, find a pset group, go to TF OH or MQC if you can early on and reach out or help before the first midterm if lost
I had a terrible relationship with math in high school, and I think math 1b helped reignite this interest in math that I thought I had lost forever. I entered this semester with a totally different concentration in mind, but after this class, I am really set on doing some sort of math for my concentration. Granted, I had a great TF, Fanhui, she is AWESOMEEEE! I also had great CAs. Unlike other TFs who are like graduate students that may have not have teaching experience before, Fanhui is a seasoned veteran since she had already have teaching experience before coming to Harvard. I believe this is a first year here, but she was so great and organized that no one could probably tell. She can answer any question asked and put math into very understandable terms. She starts out class with a review session from last class and that transitions very well into the worksheets we do during that class. The worksheet is standard across all sections. I don't know how other class sections do this, but in my section, we have a worksheet. Fanhui speaks a bit and maybe give some crucial information/hint. We are off on our own for like 5–10 minutes but the CAs and her are walking around to answer any questions. Then we regroup and she explains the problem and how to approach it. Repeat. I think this is a very effective way of learning in that students are encourage to find out things on their own but it's also not totally laissez–faire.

Before, I had a really hard time in class in high school because I was stuck with trying to memorize everything, but this class helps you know how we derive to a certain concept, so even if we are faced with a scenario or a situation we have never seen before, we can just go through it step by step like how we did when we derive the concept and still come to an answer. I think it made me more of a flexible learner in that sense because I can apply what I know readily and to anything versus memorizing something and being stuck up a creek without a paddle when faced with something a tad bit different.

Yes, there is a lot of work in this class, but it is very worth it. I personally enjoyed the psets, and there are always very great resources. Some psets may require a three hours stake out operation at the MQC but not always. This definitely was a time commitment. I am currently taking 4 stem classes and 3 of them are pset classes with one of them being this class. This class takes more time then all of my other classes combined.
