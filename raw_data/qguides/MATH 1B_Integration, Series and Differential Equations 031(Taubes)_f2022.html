The beginning of this course is challenging, but do not question your level of preparedness if you have fulfilled the prerequisites (Math 1A or placing into 1B). Eventually things will become a bit more manageable, so do not drop the course for fear of being unprepared. And putting off 1B for the next semester to "prepare" is not the way to go. Just get it over with if it is a requirement for you.

Here is some advice that would have saved me hours and hours of frustration. This is the biggest hack anyone could have told me about. When you do the Psets, try to do them in office hours or MQC. This will speed up the process and ensure you are completing the problems correctly. MQC is desolate on the days the Psets are assigned, so go those nights and complete the Pset in MQC.

The section worksheets are not a good way to learn the content. They teach the concepts in a pretty roundabout way, so be patient and do not get overwhelmed. A lot of the stuff they explore is not relevant to actually solving the problems on the Psets or the exams.

Whatever section leader you get assigned to, visit Amadeus Martin's office hours and make appointments with him if you ever need help. He is a godsent, the best really.

Overall, use the support network of peers, TAs, and instructors to your advantage. You will just not be able to figure these things out on your own––not because you aren't capable, but because the questions were designed for collaboration.
It is a grind, not something fun but it had to be taken.
Don't take this class unless you really have to. Workload is very heavy, 3 psets a week is harder than you would maybe expect, definitely go to MQC and office hours. This class is made more challenging than it has to be – for instance, no explanations were provided for the final exam practice tests, just the answer – which made it really hard to figure out some questions since you literally had no explanation for the solution. Teaching in section varies a lot, please have a TF who's teaching style fits you.

This class is really fast paced/is very rushed, sometimes I feel like I'm not learning much at all because we are going so fast, there's not much time to consolidate knowledge. I've personally had really good experiences with math classes before this class.
This class is intense but ultimately rewarding.
It's engaging but it demands a large amount of time. I would recommend that you do not fall behind on the work. MQC is key and attack the problems a day before they are due in case you have questions.
This class will give you moments of panic with late night psets, but if you stick with it everything will work out in the end.
This was overall a good class, though the psets could sometimes be a lot. Loved the collaborative aspect though and would recommend finding a pset/study group!!
Swallow your pride, and work with your peers. It's usually better to ask someone that is experiencing similar problems for help than it is to struggle on a single problem for an hour or two.
This course can definitely be challenging, but having a pset collaboration group can really help lessen the difficulty of the course. Teaching each other concepts is a great way to make sure you understand what's going on. Also, it's really important to start the psets before on your own before asking for help or checking with others; that way, you're able to evaluate how much you know and understand before getting help. To add on, time management is sooo important! Having three psets and biweekly QRDs can already be stressful, so you can make things easier for yourself by starting them early, like on the day (psets) or week (QRD) you're assigned them. Lastly, PRACTICE A LOT BEFORE MIDTERMS AND FINALS!! One great thing about this course is that a lot of practice materials and mock exams are given to us, so make sure to utilize them because they are so helpful before exams. After all, math is all about practicing and applying your knowledge. Anyways, if you decide to take this class, good luck!! :)
Never start the problem set the night before it is due. Start problem sets the day of class. The MQC is extremely crowded Sunday night. If you have a TF that you are not learning well from, switch out of the section immediately. Email your TF if you are struggling. Begin studying for exams as early as possible. Make sure to have a good understanding of basic calculus before beginning the class. Get a tutor at the beginning of the semester if needed.
