Take this class! The readings are incredibly interesting and push you to understand the world in a way that might be unfamiliar to you. You will leave with a new–and necessary–understanding of disability history and justice , and your role in developing both of them. The readings are not necessarily academically challenging/dense, but they do challenge you ethically, epistemoligically, and critically. I wish that the pedagogical standards and practices for this class were the MO of all courses at Harvard.
If you're interested in disability history, this is a great class to take. Maddie is really kind and flexible, grading is pretty easy, and there are minimal assignments. However, you might get bored at times due to how long the readings and class periods are and how disorganized it can be.
The class could be shorter.
Take this class. This is the first class that has been offered about disability studies since I came to Harvard. I am so glad that I had the opportunity to study disability justice with other students passionate about the topic. The readings are excellent, as are the guest speakers. All assignments are manageable, though there is a good deal of work, and you will learn a lot. The final project is very flexible according to what you are interested in, and I had so much fun studying areas of disability history that I care about.
Maddie is amazing! She makes the class super engaging and provides you with the tools to create accessible history that includes disabled people, disability studies theory, and body difference. I highly suggest you take this course. She makes intersectional history essential focus of the class, so if you’re interested in African–American history, disability history, Native American history, queer history, and women’s history, I would highly suggest this course. Maddie is also super accommodating. If you want a course that teaches you a lot, is super engaging, and is stress–free, I would definitely suggest this course. She asks for accessibility feedback the entire semester and provided multiple opportunities to submit anonymous feedback, so she truly integrates student feedback throughout the semester
This class is a great idea and I wish it was offered every year, but the execution could use some work. Most of the class time was spent on administrative issues rather than content, which was pretty frustrating. Regardless, this course covers things you wouldn't touch on in any other class, so if you want to learn about disability studies, definitely take it. However, if you're expecting a traditional history seminar you will be out of luck as history was on the back burner of class conversations. Also, I think now that the Professor had this trial run, the next time it's offered will be much better as many of the issues from this semester will be ironed out.
If you want to understand a historically hidden group of people (which you might be apart of) please take this class. You learn so much that alters your worldview, and have opportunities to expand the course into other passions.
This class contains a lot of thought–provoking material and tends to attract really good people. Maddie puts so much care into ensuring that the course is accessible to everyone in a way that made me mad about every *other* class I've taken! However, it's fairly disorganized, maybe as a result of this being its first year of existence (?). We never got around to discussing a lot of the readings, and the discussions that we did have sometimes felt abruptly cut short. This factor, combined with the class length (three hours, one day a week) made it feel like the timeline could be better managed.
This course is somewhat of a diamond in the rough. Its topic is amazing, and Maddie is certainly an expert in her field. But not everything works— some assignments don't fully make sense and it can feel like there's a lot of busy work at times.
I would recommend this class as it was not extremely difficult and the content of the materials was very interesting. Instructor gives very thorough and targeted feedback which was very helpful.
