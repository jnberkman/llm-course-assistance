What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is really great if you have a great TF (Natalie Ramirez!!). I personally found this class to be really enjoyable, but others were not as lucky. The course load is extremely manageable, and it's easy to balance with other classes. There are four compositions/essays (only 2 pages each with a draft and a revision), and four exams on the tenses you've learned. You practically learn a new tense every class, so you'll need to dedicate some time to stay on top of those. The only thing that takes up a lot of time is the readings for each class, and the problem isn't the length, it's the vocabulary. This class really isn't bad at all, and I highly recommend it!
Overall really fun and has a manageable workload!! It made me realize why everyone says to take a language class at Harvard :)
Natalie Ramirez has the best section.
I found the essays to be the most freeing part of the class, because you can explore writing in Spanish with your own thoughts. In my experience, I had mostly written dry paragraphs for assignments in high school, but discovered with this class and the varying prompts that I could write creatively and personally in another language. I had a fantastic instructor and reaching out to my instructor outside of class proved invaluable.
I took Spanish before college basically since Kindergarten and I still didn't pass the Spanish language placement exam before freshman year... yeah... But after taking Spanish 20 I feel like I've actually been able to understand and properly learn the language and fill in all the gaps that I had before! So definitely a great class for people who have taken Spanish in high school or before and want to fill in their gaps and learn some things from the beginning, but know enough to start at a somewhat intermediate level.
I loved this class, I would recommend to anyone with some experience with Spanish.
