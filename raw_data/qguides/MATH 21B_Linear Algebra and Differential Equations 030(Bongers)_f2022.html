What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Begin the psets early if there are still 3 a week.
This class is really really really hard and the three psets a week are relentless. I loved the material, but felt completely burnt out by the end and the amount of work built a resentment inside of me for the class that I really didn't want to have. The tests are difficult and the amount of material taught is a lot. Find a good pset group and hope for the best.
Would not take this course if it's not a requirement. The course is overwhelming and unnecessarily stressful, and the three problem sets per week are superfluous. The only way to be successful is to spend hours in office hours, which are mainly held at night.
This course requires you to learn a lot of rules in linear algebra and different formulas. I would recommend spending extra time either relearning and memorizing the rules or it will be difficult to remember on exams. Also, true or false is a big part of this course, so being able to come up with counterexamples is key on exams. Work on this if you have trouble with it.
Before taking this class I heard it was difficult, so I was preparing for the worst. However, it is NOT THAT BAD! The 21 series is great, and you have SO MANY support resources. But, even with just the class worksheets, PSet's are doable alone. The only thing that sucks is 3 PSets per week, but as long as you stay on top of them it is fine. Multiple choice on exams are really difficult, so I would recommend practicing those a bunch. THE BEST WAY TO STUDY FOR EXAMS IS TO DO THE PRACTICE EXAMS!
Be prepared for the workload––it's super easy to fall behind on material if you procrastinate on homework. I'd often start the problem sets immediately after the corresponding section just so I could get as much of it out of the way and also prepare to go to MQC if I couldn't figure out the answers. Also, I would make flashcards/a general note sheet to keep track of all the formulas and concepts, because everything is cumulative.
Having problem sets after every class was not fun. It felt a lot like high school math class. The MQCs are really helpful and I would recommend going the night that you have section instead of the night before the PSET is due to avoid a large crowd. I thought that the exams for this class were the hardest part. The PSETS were annoying to do but not always incredibly difficult especially with help from CAs. The exams were tough and kinda rolled me so I'd recommend studying early for them.
Take this class if you really enjoy math and have a background in the topics that are being taught as it will be pretty full on
