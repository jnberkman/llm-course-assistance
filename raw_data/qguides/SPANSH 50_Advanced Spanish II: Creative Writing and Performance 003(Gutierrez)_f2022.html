Really fun and engaging course. Great for getting more opportunities to speak in Spanish. I would 100% try to take it at the same time as a friend if you can because it makes it so much more enjoyable. This class is a lot more busy work than Spanish 40, but it still is pretty easy to get a good grade.
Really fun class, definitely consider taking. Helps with public speaking and performance and involves a lot of group work so you wont feel like you're doing it all on your own.
Take this class!!! I was in Natalie's section and she was an amazing professor. There was a lot of great community in my section, which is a lot to do with the environment she created. You'll definitely come out of Spanish 50 with better comprehension and speaking abilities. I did find that there were more assignments than I anticipated, and readings were somewhat long. But, the group projects are low stress and honestly pretty fun. And I actually enjoyed the readings, especially the plays.
Do it if you want a new way of approaching Spanish that doesn't necessarily sacrifice language/grammar skills. The workload isn't obscene either, but I think the benefit of this is that every lesson is full of exciting discussions and the exchange of ideas. It's a must for anyone doing Spanish
Spanish 50 is super fun and was easily one of the less stressful classes I've taken at Harvard. All the exams are group presentations that are enjoyable to do and don't take much time to prepare at all. There is the occasional essay, but it's usually only two pages and super doable. Natalie was such a great instructor and emphasized that her class should not be stressful at all. Overall, great classroom environment and you learn a lot.
Take this! It’s a lot of fun, VERY low stress, and you’ll see your Spanish improve a lot
I loved reading the various texts and interpreting them for the exams! Thoroughly enjoyed the class. Couldn't recommend it more.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!
This is def one of the best classes in the Spanish department. I did not want to take the class initially since it's advertised as a writing class but it's NOT what you think. We read plays and stories and wrote creative journals every other week. ALSO, IT HAS NO EXAMS! All the exams are group projects in which you write a scene and act it out. There is very little pressure in the class and the teachers really care about your learning and providing a safe space to practice the language. There is other small work on the side that is tedious but not hard. For the most part, it's a chill class, and maybe one week out of the month things pile up for the class.
I would tell them that they should definitely take it
