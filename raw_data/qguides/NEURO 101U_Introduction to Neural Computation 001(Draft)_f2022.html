Take this tutorial! As someone who previously had no experience with the computational side of neuroscience, I have not had much trouble understanding the important concepts. Since the focus of the class is reading papers and writing about them, you will definitely be fine coming in with a background in neurobiology. Kiah is such a wonderful lecturer –– she is incredibly kind, understanding, and enthusiastic. The assignments are very reasonable, and you will walk away with a really strong understanding of key fields within computational neuro.
You should absolutely take this class! You learn so much about the computational basis of neurobiology in a very short time and the work–load is not too heavy. This has been my favourite neuroscience class at Harvard by a long way. The assignments are short and really useful for helping to understand the material. Definitely ask questions when you can because it is a very progressive course and you need to understand the early week materials, for reading papers later on.
TAKE THIS CLASS IF YOU CAN! Very low stress and highly informative
Kiah will guide you with the papers and your learning! She is a great facilitator and if you ever need extra help reach out to her. You don’t need much background, just enough for content to make sense.
If you have prior experience with neuroscience content (at the level of Neuro 80) and are looking for a small, fun, and relatively easy neuroscience tutorial/class to dive into an aspect of neuroscience in more depth, then this course is for you. Kiah (our instructor) is a very wise and friendly instructor who has a passion for this material and will try her best to invoke a sense of wonder and curiosity in you, too. The papers you read can be dense at times by the pure nature of the material, but Kiah and the organization of the class make digesting these papers easy. Good luck and enjoy the course!
