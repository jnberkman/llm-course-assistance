Spanish 40 is definitely a good class. I enjoyed watching a discussing the movies, reading the literature, and learning more about the political history of Latin America from the 1970s onward. Fair warning that this course is more involved than previous Spanish courses. You have to do a lot more work than you initially expect. Also, the movies clearly are politically leftist. I wish there were more balance, but the course is fine as long as you go in aware of the bias. The final project, a short film in groups, was a lot of fun to make! I'd definitely recommend this course to all those remotely interested in taking it.
This course is a very good way of improving your spanish knowledge while doing so in a very engaging and entertaining way (movies, interesting readings, etc).
You have to keep up with all of the readings/movies and assignments which is a lot, but the work itself is not that difficult and the grading is very fair.
Take this class! The small class format, really engaged professors and enthusiastic classmates, and having the class both taught and spoken in Spanish truly deepens your ability to speak in Spanish, listen to Spanish, and think in Spanish.
Aina is literally the best teacher ever!!!! She is really helpful and supportive and always responsive to questions.
Spanish 40 was amazing!! I know your experience will vary depending on your TF, but I have yet to encounter a TF/professor in a Spanish class that wasn't amazing. The content is definitely more challenging than previous spanish classes, so do be prepared to study harder for the exams than past ones, but the TFs are on your side. Spanish 9am section was the best section this semester!!
This class was amazing! I highly recommend it. The variety of course materials, from movies, to essays, stories, and even songs helped give us a more complete understanding of Spanish and Latin American culture. Dr. Obis was amazing at teaching and was always understanding as we tried to learn the new language. The small class size allows for a lot of conversation which helped us improve, and the assignments were graded fairly and were often fun!
I definitely recommend this class! The professors are great, and the workload is very manageable, but you still get a lot out of the readings and movies assigned. A lot of class time is spent in big or small group discussions of the works that are being viewed, and I feel a lot more confident in my Spanish after taking this class. Also, the final project is really fun and low stress!
It's a super fun way to take a spanish class! Watching about a movie a week is super fun. The workload is very manageable.
