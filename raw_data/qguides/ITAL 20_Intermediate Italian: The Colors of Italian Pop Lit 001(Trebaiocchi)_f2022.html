This was my favorite class I took this semester, but it was also the hardest and most time consuming. You will become much better at all aspects of the Italian language: speaking, comprehension, writing, grammar, but be prepared to do some work. Each grammar concept is only briefly touched on in class and then practiced with speaking, so personal study of the grammar is helpful. But despite the workload, I found almost every assignment enjoyable, and my teacher (Valentina) was very flexible and understanding with due dates and such.
I had an excellent experience with this class, but I was lucky to have an amazing section and teacher. This class is deceptively hard, there are homework assignments after every class that can be extremely time–consuming. Tests/quizzes are fair, but the grammar is very tricky. I wish I knew before going in to this class that Italian 20 has one of the highest workloads of Italian classes at Harvard. However, the class is very good and my Italian improved quite a bit.
