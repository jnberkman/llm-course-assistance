DO NOT TAKE MATH 21B. Save yourself the tears and find another linear algebra course that fulfills your degree requirement—this course should NOT be it. I decided to take 21b because I coasted through 21a and got an A easily, and I thought I was good at math, but this course is soul–sucking at best and depression–inducing at worst. One of my friends decided to take math 22a instead of 21b, and he's going to get an A while I'm probably going to get a C+ even though we had basically the same experience in math 21a.

The final two psets (one of which was the QRD pset, which was 3–4x as long as a typical pset) were due after classes ended and during reading period, which was absolutely awful. The material and concepts covered in this course were so far–fetched that I would literally suggest you only take this course after having taken a linear algebra course in high school.

In the end, math 21b taught me that I should stop taking math classes. I still don't know how to conceptualize more than three dimensions.
