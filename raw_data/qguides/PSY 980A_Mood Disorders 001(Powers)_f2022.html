This class is really a gem. It's a great way to explore mood disorders in depth and does not require much work outside of class!
This class is very interesting, and you leave with a very strong understanding of mood disorders and thecurrent research efforts. Dr. Roberts is a very supportive professor, and the many different components of the class keep you engaged.
Very interesting but need to interested in this topic
This course was by far my favorite this semester and probably will be yours if you have personal experience with a mood disorder (either know someone with one or have struggled with one yourself). Dr. Roberts is incredibly professional and knowledgeable about what she teaches and often weaves in stories from her clinical experience when answering questions. The typical workload is incredibly manageable (low–stakes weekly reading logs) but they're still really interesting and informative. Lectures can sometimes feel a little redundant, but Dr. Roberts usually includes supplementary materials like videos, and she's very open to discussions, so it's not so much a stiff lecture usually as it is some lecture ––> someone asks a questions ––> slight discussion ––> back to lecture.
Honestly barring course structure and all that logistical stuff though, the material itself is incredibly interesting and important to learn about. At least for me personally, I've known a lot of people with mood disorders and have struggled with depression myself, and it was really powerful to be learning about these disorders, their etiology, different types of treatments, and even how they connect with broader social issues. By giving you a deeper understanding about these disorders, this course really teaches you to be a lot more compassionate with others and with yourself.
This is a fun course. It draws in a nice group of people and professor Roberts is great. It can feel a little repetitive if you've done psych 18, but it's still enjoyable and rewarding. Very manageable workload and some interesting readings.
This is a great class that I highly recommend if you enjoyed Psychopathology. There is a good balance between lecture and discussion, and the readings are all interesting and engaging. Dr. Roberts is a great discussion leader and is always willing to offer helpful feedback and answer questions. One of my favorite classes at Harvard!
Super great course. Professor Roberts was great at teaching us about Mood Disorders and Psychopathology at large, and I really appreciated how she gave us feedback on each Reading Log! She was easy to contact and was eager to help out in any way, especially for the final paper. I also loved her guest lecturers as they were people currently working in the field (she had someone from Mass Gen Hospital and they gave us a ton of insight!), and she incorporated videos into our two–hour long seminars. I wouldn't say this class is a total gem, but I highly highly encourage everyone to take it as it is relatively laidback, plus the professor is good at teaching and provides a great foundation on current research on Mood Disorders :)
This was one of my favorite classes I've ever taken, and it is certainly a gem among psychology electives! The class discussions were always thought–provoking, and Dr. Roberts made the course content interesting in many ways. She is a great lecturer, cares deeply about her students, and brings years of experience both from academia and her private practice. The weekly courseload is very manageable (usually 2–3 hours/week to read articles and write the response papers), and two "big" assignments are also totally doable with some good time–management (especially since the research paper is due during the finals week). Definitely take this class if you are in search of a great psychology elective – you won't be disappointed!
