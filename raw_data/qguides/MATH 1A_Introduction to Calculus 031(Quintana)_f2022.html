For future students, I would heavily emphasize attending MQC and finding a group to complete PSETS with. Trying to undergo Math 1A by yourself is a near–impossible task, especially with how the course is graded. Then, for PSETS, if you have any questions AT ALL on how something should be worded, ASK. The graders are very tough on even the slightest wording, so it is better to be safe than sorry!
This class is good if you have already taken a bit of calculus. You will make friends in this class as the environment is very social.
My number one piece of advice is to be prepared. You will barely take anything away from this class, especially if you already took AP Calculus AB/BC. I do have to say that the content that is taught is more complex than the general information presented in AP Calculus, but having a background in calculus is ESSENTIAL to do well in this class. The only pro to this class is that you are given the chance to retest concepts on your exams, but other than that I don't really see any benefits to this class. If you need a class to fulfill a Neuroscience or Pre–med requirement just take this and be over with math. However, if you know you need higher–level math to get credit for your major AVOID this class. It is a waste of time and taking 21A will do you better and will open you to so many more opportunities. This isn't a good introduction class to calculus and if you don't have a good background in AP Calculus go to Math MA because you will have an easier time there than here. The PSETs in this class are a bit over the top and the Course Assistants at MQC do not help at all, except for a few (but that is a very RARE occasion). Definitely find a PSET group because they will help out much more than Office Hours and the MQC. I've had friends who went to Office Hours and got wrong answers from TFs, so don't trust their word. Some TFs at Office Hours can be VERY passive–aggressive, such as Michael Nathanson. Don't be fooled by his optimism, he can be very short and indifferent to your problems. Every time I've interacted with him he doesn't help and only confuses people more, and so does Dylan Quintana. Don't get me wrong these people are great but their method of helping you is only asking more questions. I don't know in what world that helps you at all, but they see it as accomplishing their job! If you want a good TF definitely try to get Duc Vo. He teaches really well and will walk through each problem step by step if you get confused. He also is willing to show different methods of solving a single problem. He has one on one meeting times every Monday and Wednesday, so if you are stuck with a concept or want to go over your exams you can meet with him! Out of all the TFs from Math 1A, I've only heard good things about Duc, which shows a lot about the course. All these TFs are qualified to teach, but it is the structure of the class that is offputting. They need to focus on working through problems that are as tricky as those on the problem sets and actually attempt to teach the concepts necessary in understanding these topics. Having students teach each other math problems benefits no one. Math here is poorly structured and its horrible to see so many kids discouraged after one math class.

TLDR: Take this class only if you need to satisfy the math requirement. Otherwise, skip it because you don't gain much from it.
