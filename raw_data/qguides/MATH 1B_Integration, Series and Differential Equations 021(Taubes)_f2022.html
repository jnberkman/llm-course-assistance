The course has been greatly improved in the last few years. The more manageable PSETs and QRDs weren't terribly difficult and did a great job at preparing you for the exams. You will get out of this class what you put into it, so go to class and try the worksheet/board work, when studying for exams, really take the time to review the PSETs as a lot of what you do there translates directly. Overall the class has been one of my favorite math classes at Harvard.
Ensure you walk out of class understanding the concept, go to TFs, or MQC, work with friends and start your work early.
Take this class! My TF, Amadeus, was incredible in balancing walking through the topics while still allowing for individual discovery. I took this class because I felt my high school CalcBC foundation (sigma notation, Taylor/MacLaurin series) was shaky. I wanted to make sure that the rest of my Harvard math experience would have a more solid base, so I decided to take this course instead of jumping into a higher math (which I did qualify for). It was the best decision I could make for me. I feel much more confident in those shaky areas.
This class requires a significant amount of time per week if you want to do well. There are three PSETs a week that on average take somewhere between 3–4 hours each. Furthermore, I strongly recommend that you go over the in–class worksheet solutions after class ends to fully understand what was discussed in the class. While this class is a lot of work, it is completely doable since the TFs and CAs are extremely helpful in class and at the MQC. But I would highly recommend taking this class during a semester where it is your primary focus work–wise.
Make sure to find people to do work with, so that you do well in the class.

Overall a very good and well taught class. The instructor I had(Amadeus) was excellent and I left feeling much more confident in my calculus skills than I had begun with. MATH 1b does have somewhat of a negative reputation of being hard, but I think that's mostly in reference to its workload. I believe the instructors are trying to change that(we had our psets permanently shortened halfway thorough the year) and regardless if you went to MQC the psets were fairly easy to get through. Admittedly, I took this class having already taken AP CALC BC in high school and technically knew about 2/3 of the course content, so take my words with a grain of salt. The class is, however, taught in a very specific way that makes concepts somewhat initially unrecognizable from what you eventually know them as. I can tell that this is done so you have a deeper understanding/conceptualization of topics, and succeeds in this, but it also means that you can't necessarily use background knowledge to aid you.
If you already took AP Calculus BC or another variant of Calculus 2 you should not be in this class, you definitely have covered the material before and this course will not teach you anything new. If you have only taken AP Calculus AB or Calculus 1, like I personally did, I would just tell you to truly be prepared. This course is not one to take leisurely, and is a great time commitment. It takes true dedication to do well and keep up with the workload, but overall it is not that bad and you will be able to handle it if you take it seriously.
Be aware that this class takes a lot of your time outside of class.
Definitely be prepared to work A LOT
