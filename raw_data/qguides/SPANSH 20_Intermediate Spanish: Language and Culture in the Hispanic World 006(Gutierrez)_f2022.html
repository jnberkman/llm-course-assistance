This is a great intermediate Spanish class to take. It's not unmanageable, and the papers and tests are designed to help you out. I noticed my Spanish grow really quickly in just a semester. Great course to refresh and expand Spanish knowledge coming out of high school, and not too taxing especially if you have multiple PSET classes and/or classes that require lots of reading.
This class is essentially a crash–course of most of the Spanish verb tenses and some grammar rules, so you definitely don't have to be very advanced to take it. The class is very engaging, and you will definitely improve in your reading, writing, and speaking skills. You'll also learn a lot about Latin American history, and present day social issues. The work load is generally light, and the readings (poems and short stories) pretty interesting. The exams and compositions are definitely doable, and accurately assess understanding of the topic (although instructions can occasionally be unclear). The only real problem with this course is that often times discussions about social issues really dominate class time, and there was often little to no review of the grammar concepts discussed in the homework. You're definitely expected to teach yourself the content at home, and be prepared to use what you learned in class. There is also little class time dedicated to reviewing for exams, so it's definitely necessary to prepare for them independently. Carlos was a very enthusiastic and knowledgeable TF, and was generally very flexible and accommodating.
This course is a great mix of grammar and literature and while it moves at a fast pace, the work is very manageable and staying on track is super achievable. Also the assements were consistently very fair and reflective of what we learned in class.
