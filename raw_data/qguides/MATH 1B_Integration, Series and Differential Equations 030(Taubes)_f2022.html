The Math Question Center was a necessary tool for me taking this class.
This class is very well taught. Section is important to attend for both understanding the material and actually enjoying the class. There are three psets per week and occasionally an additional assignment, and they are time–consuming but not extremely tedious. There are resources like office hours and the Math Question Center (MQC) for any difficult problems or just to find people to work with. One thing I would note is that if you're taking this class because you don't feel ready for something like 21a or equivalent but have already taken a class like AP Calc BC, you don't necessarily become a whole lot better prepared for the next class in terms of content, but you do learn a lot about how math classes work here and how you are expected to do things, which is nice to learn while you are not yet being bombarded with a bunch of new content.
Be ready to do a lot of work–– it's 3 psets a week, meaning that you'll be spending at least a couple hours on each. It's helpful to start them the day they're assigned so that you don't panic the day it's due. There's also a wide range of students taking this class: for some, this will be the last math class they'll ever take, for others, it's just the first of many in their college career
