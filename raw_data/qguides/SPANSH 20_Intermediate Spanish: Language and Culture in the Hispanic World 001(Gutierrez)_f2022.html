What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is 100% taught in Spanish so be prepared for that. Listen to the recommendation and either take Spanish 10 first or already have a background in Spanish.
Make sure to ask questions when you are confused! Each day of class builds off of the other and thus, builds up to the exam. Therefore, it is essential that one solidifies one's understanding throughout the course. Other than that, I would say that this course is very important to building a solid foundation in Spanish grammar rules. Additionally, preparing for the exams is quite simple if one keeps up with the Canvas homework along with participating in class.
This is a wonderful class – it is paced well, gives a comprehensive review of grammar that you need to know and would have practiced in Spanish 10 and 11, and the units are really interesting. You add to your grammar knowledge and begin practicing integrating the grammar into conversation. The tests can be a bit difficult, but are extremely doable. It is a really rewarding class!
You will become proficient at using much more complicated sentence structures in Spanish than you have before, and will be able to express more complex ideas as a result. However, this class is very difficult, and to become proficient, you will need to put a lot of work in practicing the grammar showed in class yourself. Also be prepared for nit–picky grading, though this does provide a lot of helpful constructive feedback.
I would tell future students that this course is extremely beneficial if they want to continue learning about Spanish, and the knowledge that you gain is extremely helpful in furthering your ability to communicate with others in Spanish.
