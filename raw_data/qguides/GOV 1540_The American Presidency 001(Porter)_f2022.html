The professor has a lot of cool stories and is a nice guy, but he's also completely out of touch with the needs of students. Do not trust the due dates in the syllabus. He makes them up as the course goes along. You are expected to read several weeks ahead in the material and attend multiple office hours if you want to do well on your paper. You have to approach this course with the enthusiasm of the most annoying first–year section kid. Sections are really helpful; definitely do NOT skip.
Do all the readings!
Love this class — Professor Porter’s lectures are a highlight and give you a one–of–a–kind view into the presidency! It’s best to read ahead and take an active role in growing your understanding of the material, though — assignments don’t always correspond to the lectures they precede, and often ask about future readings. Still, this course is a lot of fun.
I wouldn't recommend taking this course. The readings are interesting, but they're virtually the only content the class has to offer. The lectures have little substance, meaning that most classes will feel like a waste of time. The assignments are interesting but given out in far too rushed a manner. You'll receive little time to complete even your drawn–out research papers, and the midterm and final are intentionally put into an unpleasant time crunch. The grading metrics for this class are arbitrary and hyperspecific, making it much more difficult to perform well than it should be. If you're interested in the Presidency, I'd just read Neustadt's "Presidential Power" book—it contains most of the content in this course but won't waste anywhere near as much of your time.
Take this class if you want to have an entertaining HKS class. The course content is sometimes confusing and not much substance behind it but overall I enjoyed this class.
Sometimes referred to as "storytime with Roger Porter," which is a good thing. Porter has many great stories to share during class, which is the primary benefit of it. Sometimes this does mean that he deviates from the outline of the formal class material, which is sometime to be warned about if you are particularly interested in the intricacies of the office of the presidency. Readings are okay, not too time–consuming. However, the main concern about this class are the assignments. There are 2 7 page papers, a take–home midterm (comprised of 2 3–page papers that must be completed within about 5 days) and a final (same structure as the midterm). This totals around 26 pages of writing, split between 6 different assignments. Moreover, the scope of each paper is incredibly broad given the limited space given to respond to them. As such, papers are difficult to write due to trying to answer the entire prompt (which you must do or else you lose points) in such a small amount of pages. Grading is not particularly lenient either (for reference, I think grading is 80% as difficult as Gov 1061). Overall, it is an interesting course but do not underestimate the difficulty of assignments and grading.
This is a good class to learn about the workings of American politics. The assignments aren't overwhelming, but you will need to do the readings and be prepared to discuss or lecture.
This class is the epitome of what it means to go to Harvard! Professor Porter is amazing! The content is great and he gives so many good stories and anecdotes! This is one of my favorite classes I took so far!
While the Professor shares interesting stories, his lectures are generally disconnected from the readings. Moreover, paper topics are expansive and page counts aren't large enough for comprehensive responses. Attendance is taken at lectures.
This class was super interesting! I would recommend it. Be prepared for the grading, though, there are no assignments besides the papers so be comfortable with writing very crisp papers. Everyone who has an interest in the presidency should take this to learn so much!
There is a lot of writing in this course, but overall I feel like I took away a lot from it.
I found this course to be very interesting and recommend it if you are interested in the topic of the presidency. Professor Porter's lectures were full of stories with takeaways from his personal experience in the White House. Most of your grade will be based on essays (including take–home essay based midterms and finals) and there are no in–person exams.
Take this class only if you really really love the American political system. Interesting anecdotes but not much discussion on content.
This course made me less partisan and more open minded. It gave me the tools necessary to evaluate American politics through a lens of competence and theory rather than ideological goals.
The lectures in this class are useless and anecdote–based. This class also has a strict grading curve: only 10% of assignments get As.
This is an AMAZING class. Professor Porter is clearly knowledgeable about the subject. Not only is he incredibly well read on the subject, he also has a wealth of practical experience that supplements that. He cares very deeply about his students and in helping them to understand the material. I sometimes wish that classes were longer so we could hear more of his stories (which he has many), but we would often run out of time. This is truly an amazing class content wise. Logistics wise, the coursework is incredibly manageable. It is very reading heavy at times, but beyond that, the assignments and exams are not super intense.
