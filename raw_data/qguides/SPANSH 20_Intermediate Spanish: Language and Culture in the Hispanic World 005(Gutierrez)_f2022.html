It's hard, but there are resources here for you. It helped me grow at an extremely accelerated rate in my communication abilities.
If you can take another language, I recommend it, I heard Asian languages are done a lot better.
I found the workload for this class to be pretty manageable. The homework assignments are not too extensive and the class is well–structured overall.
This class was very challenging and sitting through section was close to torture because the level of Spanish you are supposed to speak and understand is absurd coming from Spanish 11. From what I understood this varied from section to section.
the course will make you a better listener and writer in Spanish and you will see almost all the grammar from Spanish15 (or the two–course equivalent of that course) again in a way that allows you to solidify your understanding and apply the grammar with ease to your writing. you will get a lot of speaking practice with peers, though there is limited feedback on your speaking from the instructor and perhaps another course would be better if you're primarily interested in becoming a better Spanish speaker. i did this course as a grad student interested in becoming fluent in Spanish and felt that it did not take away from my research, so i can highly recommend it for any grad students as well! i probably spent 5 hours on the course during regular weeks and 10 hours on the course during weeks when we had both an exam and a paper due (only 4 weeks a semester), but you can get away with less too if you're caught up in a given week. sometimes assignments weren't clear, so never hesitate to ask for clarification!
Keep up to date with the material as it builds on itself.
